6.1 Visual Design Philosophy
UNIQUE & MEMORABLE AESTHETIC:
- Break away from traditional "medical blue" - create a distinctive brand identity
- Modern, approachable design that reduces dental anxiety
- Warm, welcoming atmosphere balanced with professional credibility
- Personality-driven design that tells your clinic's unique story

COLOR PALETTE (Contemporary Approach):
Option 1 - Warm & Inviting:
  * Primary: Soft terracotta, warm sage green, or muted coral
  * Secondary: Cream, warm off-white, or soft beige
  * Accent: Gold, copper, or deep teal
  * Neutrals: Charcoal gray instead of pure black

Option 2 - Fresh & Modern:
  * Primary: Deep forest green or navy blue
  * Secondary: Soft pink or peach
  * Accent: Bright mint or sunshine yellow
  * Neutrals: Warm gray tones

Option 3 - Bold & Confident:
  * Primary: Deep purple or burgundy
  * Secondary: Soft lavender or blush
  * Accent: Bright turquoise or emerald
  * Neutrals: Sophisticated gray palette

Key Principles:
- Use gradients and color transitions (subtle, not overwhelming)
- Implement glassmorphism effects for modern depth
- Organic, flowing color blocks instead of rigid rectangles
- 60-30-10 color rule (60% dominant, 30% secondary, 10% accent)

TYPOGRAPHY (2026 Trends):
- Variable fonts for dynamic, responsive typography
- Font pairing: Modern sans-serif (headings) + elegant serif or geometric sans (body)
- Large, bold headings (48-72px) for impact
- Generous line height (1.6-1.8) for readability
- Fluid typography that scales smoothly across devices
- Minimum body text: 18px (not 16px - modern accessibility standard)

Examples:
  * Headings: Plus Jakarta Sans, Satoshi, Cabinet Grotesk, or Clash Display
  * Body: Inter, DM Sans, Sora, or Crimson Pro (serif option)

IMAGERY STYLE:
- Authentic, candid photography (avoid overly staged stock photos)
- Natural lighting with warm tones
- Real patients (with consent) showing genuine emotions
- Mix of close-up details and environmental shots
- Incorporate lifestyle imagery (people smiling in daily life, not just dental chairs)
- Custom illustrations or icon sets for uniqueness
- 3D elements or micro-animations for services
- Asymmetric image layouts for visual interest

6.2 Layout & Spacing (Modern Standards)

LAYOUT PHILOSOPHY:
- Asymmetric, dynamic layouts over traditional grids
- Bento box / card-based design systems
- Floating elements with subtle shadows/depth
- Organic shapes and curved sections (wave dividers, blob shapes)
- Generous white/negative space (breathing room is luxury)
- Content-first approach - design serves the message
- Z-pattern and F-pattern layouts for optimal eye flow

SPACING SYSTEM:
- 8px base unit spacing system (8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, 128px)
- Large padding around sections (80-120px on desktop)
- Micro-interactions with appropriate spacing
- Visual hierarchy through size and space, not just color

RESPONSIVE BREAKPOINTS:
- Mobile: 375px - 767px
- Tablet: 768px - 1023px
- Desktop: 1024px - 1439px
- Large Desktop: 1440px+
- Ultra-wide: 1920px+

GRID SYSTEM:
- Fluid, flexible grid (not rigid 12-column)
- CSS Grid and Flexbox combination
- Subgrid support for nested layouts
- Asymmetric column widths for visual interest

6.7 Accessibility (WCAG 2.2 AAA Standards)

ENHANCED ACCESSIBILITY:
- Color contrast ratio minimum 7:1 (AAA standard)
- Focus indicators clearly visible (3px outline minimum)
- Skip to main content link
- Alt text for all images (descriptive, not generic)
- ARIA labels for interactive elements
- Keyboard navigation fully functional
- Screen reader tested and optimized
- Captions for all video content
- Transcripts for audio content
- Resizable text up to 200% without breaking layout
- No content that flashes more than 3 times per second

INCLUSIVE DESIGN:
- Dark mode option (system preference detection)
- Font size adjuster (AA, A, AAA options)
- Reduced motion mode (respects prefers-reduced-motion)
- High contrast mode option
